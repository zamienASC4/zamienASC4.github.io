Reporting an Incident

If You/or someone is in immediate danger (from a law enforcement officer) do not hesitate to call 911.|Even if you are in dangerous situation with a law enforcement officer, it is still the duty of all law enforcement to protect the rights of everyone regardless of color. Not only does calling 911 have potential to save someone’s life depending on the situation it helps make the system better by alerting the officer’s seniors who will hopefully address the situation.

If You believe your rights have been violated follow the steps below to report an incident.

1. Police Misconduct

    Common examples of police misconduct includes:

  • Excessive/unnecessary force
  • Offensive language
  • Inappropiate stop and frisk
  • Sexual Assualt

2. Document Everything

  • Write down every detail that you remember about the encounter. Even if you do not thing that it is important still write it down because you never know what can be used in your defense/case. Try to replay the events slowly in your head to remember as many details as possible. Try to be as specific as possible and quote as close to the exact words spoken.
  • If there were witnesses present, try to get their contact information including their name, phone number, and any other additional information that may help you get in touch.
  • Even if you do not obtain the officer(s) badge number, try to write down a description of the officer as badge numbers can always be obtained based off descriptions.
  • The sooner you start documenting, the better you will remember details and the stronger your argument will be. Law enforcement misconduct can be very traumatizing which can lead to memory problems or inaccuracies.

3. Filing a report

  • If you are trying to sue or if you are not charged with a crime, proceed to file. If you try to sue after you filed a report then the officer (and his union) will know what they are fighting against.
  • Find your local citizen review board and file the necessary papers.
  • Locate your local police department internal affairs department and file the necessary papers.
  • If filing a report requires mailing it in, send the form by certified mail. If the form is only obtainable by going to the station in person, DO NOT DISCUSS THE COMPLAINT WITH ANYONE as officers may harass you or tip off the officer in question.
  • It is important to note that filing a report does not guarantee a speedy process or guarantee an official investigation.
